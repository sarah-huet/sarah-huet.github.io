Professional Experience
Post-doc in Agri-food Economics
CESAER lab, INRAE, Institut Agro, France
PI: Valentin BELLASSEN
2023 – Present
→ Inferring agricultural practices and computing environmental footprint from FADN

Developed the BVIAS model (Biodiversity Value Increment from Agricultural Statistics), which estimates the biodiversity impact of food products from accountancy data and public statistics, calibrated on in situ data from the literature. Applied matching procedures on >5,500 French farms to compare Food Quality Schemes to conventional counterparts, showing that product-type differences outweigh quality-scheme effects.

Currently validating the FADN2Footprint R package, a life-cycle-assessment-inspired tool that infers farm practices from the Farm Accountancy Data Network and public statistics, and computes multi-criteria footprints at country, NUTS2, farm, activity and product scales:

  • Environmental: GHG emissions, Biodiversity Impact, Water Pollution.
  • Economic: Price premium, Net Return of Assets, Net Return of Land, Operating margin.
  • Social: Employment, Wage level, Net revenue per unsalaried working unit.
PhD in Microbial Ecology
Agroecology department, Univ. of Bourgogne France-Comté, France
PI: Laurent PHILIPPOT
2019 – 2022
→ Deciphering Biotic Interactions and their role in Soil Microbial Community Assembly and Functions
Using an innovative experimental approach and a range of statistical techniques (linear, generalized linear and mixed models, multivariate analysis, network inference, and multi-dataset integration), we causally infer the specific effects of biotic interactions, spatial distancing and community characteristics on both microbial community assembly and functions. Our approach could become a standard tool to test ecological theories and to design new microbial engineering strategies.
