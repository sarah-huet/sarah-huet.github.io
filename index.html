About Me

I am a postdoctoral researcher at INRAE (CESAER lab, Dijon, France), working at the crossroads of agricultural economics and environmental science. My current work focuses on modeling agricultural practices from accountancy data and assessing their environmental footprint — biodiversity, GHG emissions, water consumption and pollution.

Before turning to economics, I completed a PhD in microbial ecology, and before entering academia I worked as a cook, a nutritionist, and a farm worker. This diverse path gives me a unique farm-to-fork perspective on food system transitions.
